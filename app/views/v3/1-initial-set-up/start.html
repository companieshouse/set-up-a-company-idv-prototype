Register your company

Use this service to register your company with Companies House. You will be registered for Corporation Tax at the same time.

There’s a different way to register for Corporation Tax using an agent if you’ve already registered with Companies House.

Identity verification

Company directors

All company directors need to have verified their identity for Companies House before the company can be registered. This is to confirm that they are who they say they are. 

Verify your identity for Companies House (opens in a new tab).

When each director has successfully verified their identity, we will give them a Companies House personal code.

You'll need to provide each director’s Companies House personal code as part of this registration, so that we can connect their verified identity to our records.

People with significant control (PSCs)

All PSCs of a company need to verify their identity with Companies House.

You should provide their Companies House personal code as part of this registration, if you have it.

If you do not, the PSC will have 14 days from the company's incorporation date to provide their code.

What identity verification is needed if the PSC is a company

If the PSC is a company, we refer to it as a relevant legal entity (RLE).

RLEs need to provide details of a relevant officer, who will need to verify their identity with Companies House.

Find out more about who can be a relevant officer.

As part of this registration, we'll ask for their:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • Companies House personal code

If you do not provide these details as part of the registration, the RLE will have 28 days from the company's incorporation date to provide its relevant officer's details.

What company information you need to provide

When registering a company, you’ll need to provide details about all its directors, company secretaries and people with significant control. This includes:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • address
  • nationality
  • job title

Using this service

Registering a company costs £12 and can be paid by debit or credit card. Your company is usually registered within 24 hours.

In this service, you can also:

  • register for PAYE to tell HMRC you’re employing staff (including yourself if you’re the only director)
  • continue an application if you’ve already started registering

If you do not want to use ‘limited’ in your company name you must register by post.

Once the company is registered, you’ll get a ‘certificate of incorporation’. This confirms the company legally exists and shows the company number and date of formation.

You’ll need to create a Government Gateway user ID and password for your company when you register it. You cannot use your personal Government Gateway ID.

Other ways to register

You can also register:

  • by post
  • using an agent
  • using third-party software

You’ll also need to register for Corporation Tax within 3 months of starting to do business.

Register by post

You can register by post using form IN01.

Postal applications take 8 to 10 days and cost £40 (paid by cheque made out to ‘Companies House’).

Send your application to the address on the form.
