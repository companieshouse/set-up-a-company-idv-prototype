You're about to set up the Persons with Significant Control over the company

A person with significant control (PSC) is an individual or company that has at least one of the following:

  • more than 25% of the company's shares and voting rights
  • the right to appoint or remove directors
  • the right to make decisions of influence decisions that affect the company
Who is the PSC when shareholders have equal ownership?

If 2 shareholders own half of the company each, both shareholders are in control of the company equally and both are considered a PSC. This is because they each own more than 25% of shares.

If 3 shareholders have equal ownership of the company, each shareholder is in control of the company equally and each shareholder is considered a PSC. This is because they all own more than 25% of shares.

Providing PSC information

Based on the shareholders you've told us about, we have some information about the company's PSCs. We'll guide you through the process of giving us the rest.

The company may not have any PSCs if none of the shareholders own more than 25% of its shares. You'll still need to confirm this.

Identity verification

All individual PSCs must have verified their identity for Companies House.

They can do this using the Verify your identity for Companies House service (opens in a new tab).

When they’ve successfully verified their identity, we’ll give them a Companies House personal code.

You’ll need to provide each PSC's personal code as part of this registration, so that we can connect their verified identity to our records.
What identity verification is needed if the PSC is a company

If the PSC is a company, we refer to it as a relevant legal entity (RLE).

We'll need some details about the RLE's relevant officer.

This includes their:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • Companies House personal code

The relevant officer must be a director of the entity, or someone whose roles and responsibilities correspond to that of a company director. They must be aged 16 or over.

Information about the relevant officer will not be shown on the public register.
