You're about to set up the people with significant control over the company

Based on the shareholder information you've already given, we know some information about the company's people with significant control (PSCs). We'll guide you through the process of giving us the rest.

If none of the shareholders in the company own more than 25% of its shares, the company may not have a PSC. You'll still need to confirm this.

What is a person with significant control?

A PSC is an individual or company that has at least one of the following:

  • more than 25% of the company's shares and voting rights
  • the right to appoint or remove directors
  • the right to make decisions of influence decisions that affect the company

Identity verification

All PSCs need to verify their identity with Companies House. We'll then give them a Companies House personal code.

If a PSC has already verified their identity, and you have their personal code, you should provide it in this section so we can link their verified identity to our records.

Otherwise, the PSC will need to verify their identity and give us with their personal code within 14 days of the company's incorporation date.
