All directors now need to verify their identity

Directors and persons of significant control (PSCs) are now required to verify their identity as part of registering a new company. If there are multiple directors, they must all do this in advance of registering the company. PSCs can verify up to 14 days after the company is incorporated.

What is a PSC

A person with significant control (PSC) is someone who owns or controls your company. They’re sometimes called ‘beneficial owners’.

Most PSCs are those who hold:

  • more than 25% of shares in the company
  • more than 25% of voting rights in the company
  • the right to appoint or remove the majority of the board of directors
